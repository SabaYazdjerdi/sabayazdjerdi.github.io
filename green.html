Remembering the Green Movement

A new opposition was born after the disputed June 12, 2009, presidential election that changed the face of Iranian politics and Iran. The movement was widely seen as a new nonviolent, nonutopian, and populist paradigm of revolution that infused twenty-first century Internet technology with people street power. Over the next six months, the Green Movement evolved from a mass group of angry voters to a nationwide force demanding the democratic rights originally sought in the 1979 revolution, rights that were hijacked by radical clerics.
